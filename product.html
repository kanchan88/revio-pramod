The 17 Pro is the most quietly confident iPhone in years.

Apple hasn't reinvented the wheel. It's just made the wheel smoother, faster, and better at photographing itself in the rain.

Sarah Chen · Senior Editor
Tested 28 days · 42 hours of use · lab-measured

There's a pattern with Apple's Pro phones: the S-year version polishes what the previous one started, and if you squint, the differences can feel small. The iPhone 17 Pro is one of those years — but it's also the one where the pieces finally click. The A19 Pro delivers the kind of sustained performance that makes the "real laptop" comparisons feel less like marketing and more like math.

Design & build

The titanium mid-frame returns, this time wrapped around a more honest camera plateau that extends across the back. It's still a solidly dense phone at 199 g, but the weight is better distributed than last year's model, and the new matte finish resists fingerprints far better in humid conditions.

"The 17 Pro is what you buy when you want a phone to simply get out of the way."

Camera

The 48MP main sensor carries over, but the new variable aperture and a retrained image pipeline give low-light shots a noticeably cleaner look. Night-mode portraits — traditionally the iPhone's softest genre — now rival what Google has been doing on the Pixel side, with better skin-tone rendering and cleaner separation in the bokeh.

Performance & battery

In our standardized sustained-load test (30 minutes of 3D rendering at 60fps cap), the A19 Pro held 94% of peak performance. No Android flagship this year came within 10 points of that. Battery, however, is where the Pro falls short of the Pro Max: 14 hours of mixed use versus 17+ on the larger sibling, and charging remains capped at a conservative 30W wired.

Community Q&A

Does the 17 Pro work with MagSafe Duo chargers from 2021?

Answered by Sarah Chen (editor) · 3 days ago

Yes — MagSafe is backwards compatible, but you'll be capped at 15W on older pucks. The new 25W MagSafe requires Apple's 2026 charger.

Will the 17 Pro's camera tricks come to the regular 17 via software?

Answered by Revio Community · 1 week ago

Some of them, probably — notably the new Night portrait pipeline. Variable aperture stays Pro-only because it's hardware.
